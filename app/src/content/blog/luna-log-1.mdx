# Programming Languages: a Taxonomy

There exists four distinct kinds of programming languages:

| Name | Read? | Write? | Buggy? | Agentic Coding? | Examples | Result |
|-|-|-|-|-|-|-|
| Extremely Restrictive | Hard | Hard | Least Buggy | Good | Rust... that's it | Good for AI, but a huge pain in the ass overall. Slow velocity across the board, AI included |
| Somewhat Restrictive | Easy | Medium | Medium | Good, not great | C#, Java Go, Modern PHP | Result: easy overall, but many of these languages fall into OOP-isms. They also miss simple garauntees around concurrency and safety. |
| Not Restrictive | Hard | Easy | Pretty Buggy | Baaad | Old PHP, JS, Ruby | Your program will not work if its over 100 lines. You will be chasing the same bugs over and over again. AI will struggle to expand your codebas safety due to the dynamic nature of the language. Good luck! |
| Jesus Christ Save Me | Hard | Hard | HA! | Give it up | C, C++ | Your program will not work if its over 10 lines. It might seem like it works - it doesn't. You might get the appearance of a type system - you don't. Your code will have bugs so invisible they can lay around for 20 years. |

Yeah, this table is tongue-in-cheek. Of course there's more stuff out there, but I think really most will fall into one of these categories.
